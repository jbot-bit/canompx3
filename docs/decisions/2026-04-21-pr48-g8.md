## Mechanism — order-flow stop cascade

**Local extract — `chan_2013_ch7_intraday_momentum.md` p.155-157:**

> "The triggering of stops. Such triggers often lead to the so-called breakout strategies. ... The execution of these stop orders often leads to momentum because a cascading effect may trigger stop orders placed further away from the open price as well."

**Mapping to PR #48:**
- High rel_vol at ORB-close = elevated participation at the break boundary.
- Participation at a breakout level correlates with stop-orders accumulating at that level.
- More accumulated stops → stronger cascade when the level breaks → stronger continuation.
- Predicts monotonic-up: higher rel_vol → stronger expected continuation regardless of absolute level.

**Local extract — `fitschen_2013_path_of_least_resistance.md` p.34 + p.41 + Table 3-8:**

> "Clearly the path of least resistance in the commodity world is up." (p.34)
> "In the case of commodities, both daily bars and hourly bars have a tendency to trend." (p.41)
> "stocks: counter-trend on daily, trend-follow on intraday." (Table 3-8, p.41)

**Mapping:** establishes intraday trend-follow premise for BOTH commodity (MGC) AND stock-index (MNQ, MES) instruments. Explains why a single monotonic-up participation shape can hold across asset classes.
