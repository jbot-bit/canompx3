## Implied Sharpe (rough, per Bailey-LdP SR conventions)

For a within-lane rank regression, the t-stat of the rank coefficient relates to the effect size; to bridge to deployment-relevant Sharpe we'd need per-trade-R compounded over time. The **PR #48 study does NOT claim a deployment Sharpe** — it claims a shape universality (monotonic-up). DSR in this study is best interpreted against the K=3 shape-family.

## K = 3 DSR argument (cross-instrument pooled)

**K = 3 (independent replications across MNQ, MES, MGC).** Per Bailey-LdP 2014 Eq. 2:

> DSR = Φ((SR − E[SR_K]) · √(T − 1) / √(1 − γ_3·SR + (γ_4 − 1)/4 · SR²))

- E[SR_K] at K = 3: very small (≈ 0.85 · σ_SR for max of 3 IID).
- All three t-stats far exceed the K = 3 expected-max under null.
- Skewness γ_3 and kurtosis γ_4 of rank-residuals are bounded (ranks are uniform).

**Qualitative verdict:** with effect sizes of |t|>7 and K=3, DSR under conventional priors (γ_3 ≈ 0, γ_4 ≈ 3 for symmetric heavy-tail) clears 0.95 with wide margin. Precise numerical DSR computation requires gold-db access (raw per-trade pnl_r series per instrument), deferred — see open item below.

## N̂ (effective-N) for cross-instrument pooling

Per Appendix A.3 Eq. 9: N̂ = N / (1 + (N−1)·ρ̄), where ρ̄ is the average pairwise correlation of the strategies in the family.

**Cross-instrument correlation of `rel_vol` effects:**
- PR #48's 3 instruments (MNQ / MES / MGC) span equity-indices (MNQ, MES highly correlated ~0.7+) and metals (MGC ~0.2 to equities).
- Conservative ρ̄ ≈ 0.4 across the 3-instrument family.
- N̂ = 3 / (1 + 2·0.4) = 1.67 effective trials.
- Adjusted E[SR_K] bound is even tighter at N̂=1.67 than at K=3 → DSR margin improves.

PR #51 (MNQ K=105 BH-FDR survivors) was demoted by DSR at family K=105. PR #48 is K=3 (or N̂=1.67), **not K=105**. The DSR arithmetic is categorically different. PR #48's K is small by design — it's a pre-registered 3-instrument replication, not a multiplicity search.
